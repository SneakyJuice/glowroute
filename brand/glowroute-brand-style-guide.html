06 ƒ?" Imagery & Photography

Visual Style

Clean studio aesthetic. Soft lighting. Real clinic environments. No stock-photo scrubs. Every image should feel like it belongs in a luxury editorial.

ƒo" Use

  • Soft, warm studio lighting on white or cream backgrounds
  • Real clinic environments ƒ?" marble, clean surfaces, branded spaces
  • Elegant hands, serums, tools with intentional composition
  • Patients or models with natural, confident expressions ƒ?" not "before/after" energy
  • Botanical elements ƒ?" dried florals, minimal greenery
  • Monochrome or champagne-tone flat lays of skincare products

ƒo- Avoid

  • Generic stock-photo nurses or medical stock imagery
  • Bright, clinical fluorescent lighting
  • Before/after procedure imagery in brand contexts
  • Cluttered, busy backgrounds
  • Cool-toned blue/grey color grading
  • Price-focused promotional graphics or sale imagery

07 ƒ?" Spacing & Layout

Design Principles

Luxury breathes. Never pack content. Generous whitespace is a feature, not wasted space.

Spacing Scale

Layout Rules

1

Generous whitespace ƒ?" luxury breathes. Never pack content.

2

Dark hero sections with Deep Onyx or rich gradient for above-the-fold.

3

Gold (#C9A96E) as the accent ƒ?" CTAs, borders, star ratings, badges.

4

Serif for emotion, sans-serif for function. Headlines feel editorial.

5

One hero, one action, one story per section. Strip the page.
